Panic prepping prior to a significant winter storm... Historic wind gusts and snowfall. Not just any snow, heavy, wet snow.

The fear of course, is the power grid. How do you prepare for that? You minimize the effect with a generator. No generator? You supplement for the loss.

But how do you supplement for power loss in 30 degree weather? It feels futile to think about. We are completely and entirely dependent on our electrical grid from communication, warmth, and nourishment.

We won't have any way to keep our house from being cold.

No way to heat up our food.

No way to keep our perishable food from spoiling.

The messaging from our energy company so far is that restoration could take days, not hours.

I have a decent supply of non-perishable, no prep food. Plenty of blankets and warm clothes. A power bank to recharge our phones. A full tank of gas in my car. Ideally this is enough for the time we will be waiting for the power to be restored. 

There's some partial relief knowing my son has some environmental resilience thanks to his Waldorf schooling. Although the teachers "cook" the class inside with their outdoor clothes on, immediately after that period, they spend every morning hiking in the woods for an hour, no matter the temperature and precipitation. 

The last storm we had dumped 15 inches of snow on us, but that one was the perfect storm you could ask for. Cold, no wind, big fluffy flakes. Snow blowing was a breeze and it was sunny the following day. Snow removal felt arduous at the time, but by this time tomorrow, I'll be remembering it as a breeze:

![[IMG_6051.jpg]]

This was one of those fantasy snow storms where all I had to worry about was clearing our driveway and waiting for the plows to clear the roads. I knew going into it that our power would be fine, so we had a day of hot cocoa and movies to look forward to. This is thanks to the ratio of moisture to snow. That storm's ratio was 1 inch of water to 15 inches of snow. This one's quite different, with a ratio of 7:1, almost twice as heavy. This spells havoc on wires, branches, and the body being so much heavier in the shovel.

Not so much this time around. I am truly uncertain what tomorrow has in store for us. And I'm running out of actionable items to take care of today to prepare.
